Storytelling is mother of all stories. Before writing them we must learn to see and understand them.
Joseph Campbell was an american mythologist.
He wrote The Hero with a Thousand Faces, in which he presents his theory of the hero archetype he called the monomyth
Stories have all a hero story in common. The hero is who gives up their ego for a greater cause.
The basic structure of a hero story is essentially

  1. character departing
  2. forcing through struggle
  3. coming back

A story could start with an internal or external deside, that can be a want or a need.
Quests are spiritual or physical.
Cambpell conceived of a hero life cycle/hero's journey. 12 steps of the hero's journey:
  1. ordinary world/status quo
  2. call to adventure
  3. refusal of the call
  4. meeting the mentor
  5. entering the unknown/crossing the threshold
  6. test, allies and enemies
  7. approaching the cave
  8. surviving the ordeal
  9. reward
  10. the road back
  11. resurrection/final test
  12. protagonist returns changed


The Archetypes behind structures

February 25, 2026

*... The seven logic plots - Cristopher Booker, 2004

Rags to Riches → an underdog gains something they lack and loses it, and then wins it back again
The Quest → the protagonist must reach a certain location, attain objects, fulfill objectives while conquering obstacles
Rebirth → rooted in religion, it is a character changing their ways and becomes a better person
Overcoming the Monster → rooted in ancient classics, it is a hero that has to conquer evil forces. It is physical but can be internal. Has a happy ending
Comedy → involves humorous irony, a happy ending and the audience knows more than the characters
Tragedy → a protagonist is subject to faith or consequences of a critical flaw
Voyage and return → the protagonist is sent to another land/world and comes back changed


Aristotel divides stories in 3 acts: beginning, middle, end. John Yorke says that all stories can be broke into 5 sections:

  1. equilibrium
  2. disruption
  3. escalation
  4. crisis
  5. resolution


The plot in storytelling is called fabula.
The story is the way the fabula is presented, it unfolds and how it is told.
A story allows for manipulations to the presentation of the fabula, such as

  • point of view or perspective of the events
  • time manipulation - making events last longer, shorter or freezing events during narration
  • sequential ordering - narrating events non sequentially
  • span - how much time passes between events

The story does not include the meaning and interpretation.


Definitions & terms

monomyth → the hero archetype theorized by Joseph Campbell
fabula → It is the chronological sequence of events that took place. It is always logical and ordered chronologically.
It can be thought of as the "raw materials" for a story
